\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this paper, we investigated the representational similarity across layers in deep Multi-Layer Perceptrons. Using Centered Kernel Analysis (\cka), we demonstrated that adjacent layers are inherently similar, confirming that individual layers perform incremental transformations. We quantified the ``transformation capacity'' of these layers and showed that it scales with layer width and is further utilized through the training process. Our findings suggest that the internal representations of MLPs are highly constrained by their architectural parameters, with wider layers allowing for more significant representational drift. These results provide new insights into the redundancy of deep models and offer a quantitative framework for future research into model compression and architectural optimization.
